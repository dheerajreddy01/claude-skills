## Investment Decision Checklist

### Pre-Purchase Confirmation

```markdown
## Investment Decision Checklist

### Business Model
- [ ] Understand how the company makes money
- [ ] What is its competitive advantage (moat)
- [ ] What is the industry outlook

### Financial Health
- [ ] ROE > 15% for 5 consecutive years
- [ ] Operating cash flow consistently positive
- [ ] Reasonable debt ratio (< 60%)
- [ ] Dividends paid consistently

### Reasonable Valuation
- [ ] P/E below historical average
- [ ] PEG < 1 or close to 1
- [ ] DCF calculation shows a margin of safety

### Risk Assessment
- [ ] Understand the main risk factors
- [ ] Set a stop-loss point
- [ ] Investment amount doesn't exceed what you can afford to lose
```

## Financial Statement Reading Guide

### Recommended Reading Order

1. **Management Discussion & Analysis (MD&A)** - Understand company strategy and challenges
2. **Cash Flow Statement** - Actual cash movement
3. **Balance Sheet** - Company's financial condition
4. **Income Statement** - Profitability performance
5. **Footnotes** - Accounting policies and important information

### Income Statement Focus Points
- Revenue growth rate (YoY, QoQ)
- Gross margin trend
- Operating expense ratio
- Nature of non-operating income

### Balance Sheet Focus Points
- Current ratio > 1.5
- Quick ratio > 1.0
- Debt-to-equity ratio
- Goodwill as a percentage of total assets

### Cash Flow Statement Focus Points
- Whether operating cash flow is positive
- Operating cash flow vs net income comparison
- Capital expenditure trend
- Free cash flow situation

## Earnings Quality Check

```markdown
## Earnings Quality Assessment

High-quality earnings:
- Operating cash flow > net income (cash conversion rate > 100%)
- Low accruals
- High predictability

Low-quality earnings:
- One-time gains (asset disposals, FX gains)
- Changes in accounting policy
- Large accruals
```

## Quantitative Screening Criteria

### Value Investing Screen
- P/E < 15
- P/B < 1.5
- Dividend yield > 3%
- Debt-to-equity ratio < 50%
- 5 consecutive years of profitability

### Growth Investing Screen
- Revenue growth rate > 15%
- EPS growth rate > 15%
- ROE > 15%
- Gross margin > 30%
- Positive price momentum

### GARP (Growth at a Reasonable Price) Screen
- PEG < 1.5
- EPS growth rate > 10%
- ROE > 15%
- Debt-to-equity ratio < 100%

## Dividend Investing Analysis

### Dividend Evaluation Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Good Standard |
|------|------|----------|
| **Dividend Yield** | Annual Dividend / Share Price | 3-6% |
| **Payout Ratio** | Dividend / EPS | 40-70% |
| **Dividend Growth Rate** | This year's dividend growth % | Steady growth |
| **Consecutive Years of Dividends** | Years of continuous payment | > 10 years |

> **Yield trap warning**: an unusually high dividend yield (e.g., >8%) more often signals a falling share price pricing in distress than a genuine bargain — check whether free cash flow still covers the dividend before assuming the yield is sustainable.

### Dividend Growth Model (Gordon Model)

```
Intrinsic Value = D₁ / (r - g)

D₁ = next year's expected dividend
r  = required rate of return
g  = dividend growth rate

Example:
Dividend $2, growth rate 5%, required return 10%
Value = 2 / (0.10 - 0.05) = $40
```

### Dividend Investing Checklist
- [ ] 10+ consecutive years of dividend payments
- [ ] Dividends growing year over year
- [ ] Payout ratio < 70%
- [ ] Free cash flow covers the dividend
- [ ] Reasonable debt ratio

## Behavioral Finance Traps

| Bias | Description | Countermeasure |
|------|------|------|
| **Anchoring** | Fixated on the purchase price | Reassess intrinsic value |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Only seeking information that supports your own view | Deliberately seek out counterarguments |
| **Herding** | Buying/selling because everyone else is | Stick to independent analysis |
| **Loss Aversion** | Disliking losses more than liking gains | Set a stop-loss and follow through |
| **Overconfidence** | Overestimating your own judgment | Record and review your decisions |
| **Recency Bias** | Overweighting recent performance | Look at long-term data |
