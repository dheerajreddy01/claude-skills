## Investing & Financial Planning

### Investment Vehicle Comparison

| Vehicle | Risk | Return Potential | Liquidity | Best for |
|------|------|----------|--------|------|
| **CDs (Certificates of Deposit)** | Very low | 1-2% | Medium (locked until maturity) | Emergency reserve |
| **Bonds/ETFs** | Low | 3-5% | High | Stable allocation |
| **Stock ETFs** | Medium | 7-10% | High | Long-term investing |
| **Individual stocks** | High | Variable | High | Requires research skill |
| **Cryptocurrency** | Very high | Very high/very low | High | High risk tolerance |
| **Real estate** | Medium | 5-10%+ | Low | Long-term asset |

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts (U.S.)

| Account | Contribution Limit (2026) | Tax Treatment | Best for |
|------|------|------|------|
| **401(k)/403(b)** | $24,000 (employee, +catch-up if 50+) | Pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth) | Employer match first — it's free money |
| **Traditional IRA** | $7,000 | Tax-deductible contributions, taxed on withdrawal | Deferring taxes to a lower-bracket retirement |
| **Roth IRA** | $7,000 (income limits apply) | After-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawal | Younger earners expecting higher future tax rates |
| **HSA** | $4,300 individual / $8,550 family | Triple tax-advantaged (deductible, grows tax-free, tax-free for medical) | Anyone with a high-deductible health plan |

> Contribution limits are set annually by the IRS and adjust for inflation — verify current-year figures before planning.

### Asset Allocation Principles

```markdown
## Financial Pyramid

1. **Emergency fund** (6 months of living expenses)
   └─ High liquidity, available anytime

2. **Insurance coverage**
   └─ Medical, accident, life insurance

3. **Stable investments** (50-70%)
   └─ ETFs, bonds, dollar-cost averaging

4. **Growth investments** (20-40%)
   └─ Individual stocks, sector ETFs

5. **Speculative allocation** (5-10%)
   └─ Cryptocurrency, emerging markets

## Simple Rules
- 100 - age = stock allocation percentage
- Don't invest in what you don't understand
- Diversify and hold for the long term
```

### Cryptocurrency Basics

```markdown
## Cryptocurrency Fundamentals

### Major Coins
| Coin | Type | Use case |
|------|------|------|
| BTC | Store of value | Digital gold |
| ETH | Smart contract platform | DeFi, NFT |
| USDT/USDC | Stablecoin | Pegged to USD |

### Investment Strategy
1. **DCA (dollar-cost averaging)** - The safest approach
2. **Long-term holding** - Don't watch short-term volatility
3. **Strict stop-loss** - Set an amount of loss you can tolerate
4. **Diversify** - No more than 10% of your portfolio

### Risk Reminders
⚠️ High volatility — could go to zero
⚠️ Many scams — be careful
⚠️ Watch out for tax implications — the IRS treats crypto as property; every sale, swap, or crypto-for-goods transaction is a taxable event
⚠️ Only invest money you can afford to lose
```

### Pricing Strategy

```markdown
## Pricing Methods

### Hourly Rate
- Best for: Projects with unclear scope
- Drawback: Income has a ceiling

### Project-Based Pricing
- Best for: Projects with clear scope
- Calculation: (Estimated hours × hourly rate) × 1.5 buffer

### Value-Based Pricing
- Best for: Projects where value can be quantified
- Calculation: 10-20% of the value created for the client

## Pricing Mindset
- Quote with confidence
- Explain the value, not just the price
- Offer options (basic/standard/premium)
- Better to quote high and get negotiated down than to quote too low
```

## Avoiding Traps

| Trap | Reminder |
|------|------|
| Get rich quick | There's no such thing as a free lunch |
| High return, low risk | Doesn't exist — watch out for scams |
| Borrowing to invest | Absolutely never do this |
| Following the crowd | Do your own research |
| Ignoring taxes | Side income must be reported to the IRS; freelance/1099 income above $400 net also triggers self-employment tax — set aside 25-30% and consider quarterly estimated payments |
